Booster for the commons: community organizer, open source strategist, web developer, and speaker.

Josh Simmons is a community organizer, open source strategist, and civic engagement geek based in California's North Bay Area. He's been a freelancer, startup CEO (RIP), web developer, marketer, and campaign manager, and spends much of his time organizing in both a professional and volunteer capacity.

Josh currently works as Senior Principal Foundations Advocate at Tidelift, leads the Public Health Pledge initiative, and serves as Operations Lead for Petaluma Pride, co-organizer of North Bay Python, and as an appointed member of the City of Petaluma's General Plan Advisory Committee and Technology Advisory Committee.

Recognized as an advocate for inclusive community building and tackling systemic issues across many contexts, Josh is best known as a respected member of the worldwide open source software community. Josh served as President and Chair of the Open Source Initiative's board. During his six-year tenure he oversaw a restructuring of the board and staff to better serve the organization's members and the open source ecosystem.

More than anything else, though, Josh is a small town cat dad and happy husband. A queer disabled man and an outdoorsman. A concert goer and aspiring aviator. He dreams of going on sabbatical to pursue research, and daydreams about disappearing into the forest.

You can find Josh on Mastodon, Pixelfed, BookWyrm, GitHub, and LinkedIn.
